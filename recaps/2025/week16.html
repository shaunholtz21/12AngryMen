🏆 ROAD TO THE ANGRY BOWL 🏆

Round 16 • Winners Bracket

Four teams remain in the hunt for the Angry Bowl, the bracket finally converging as the byes expire. Shoulda Played The Other Guy and Dirty Beers now meet the challengers who fought their way through Round 15 Team Baune and HuPhers. Two teams will punch their ticket to the Angry Bowl. Two will see their run end one step short.

The Road to the Angry Bowl begins with survival.

HuPhers (109.94) vs. 💥 SHOULDA PLAYED THE OTHER GUY (142.62)

Result: Shoulda Played The Other Guy wins by 32.68
Mood: One team delivered nuclear star power. The other watched its core collapse at the worst possible time.

HuPhers came in with firepower but saw the foundation crumble early. Lamar Jackson (4.74) delivered a stunning dud, Jacobs (4.8) never got going, and Parkinson (4.1) added to the drag. Bright spots came from Chase (19.9), JSN (23.6), Hampton (16.5), and a massive Saints D/ST performance (19.0), but the lineup lacked the explosive punch needed to keep pace. Even Lutz (9.0) and Dowdle (8.3) couldn’t close the widening gap.

Shoulda Played The Other Guy unleashed a playoff masterpiece. Puka Nacua detonated for 46.5 — the kind of week that ends seasons — while Henry (22.8) and Gainwell (23.8) bulldozed through their matchups. Reichard (11.0) and the Texans D/ST (11.0) added stability, and even with quiet outings from McBride (6.7), Egbuka (5.0), and Wan’Dale (5.2), the ceiling was simply too high to match.

When Puka goes supernova, the matchup is already over.

Bench Regrets:
HuPhers: Diggs (22.8), Stevenson (17.8), and Juwan Johnson (16.9) were strong options — but none were enough to erase a 30+ point deficit.
Shoulda Played The Other Guy: Walker III (25.4), Lawrence (31.16), and Johnston (20.4) were massive bench eruptions, but the win was already locked.

Shoulda Played The Other Guy advances behind one of the most dominant WR performances of the postseason. HuPhers had talent on the field, but the QB collapse and FLEX struggles proved fatal. In Round 16, margins shrink — and this one wasn’t close.

💥 TEAM BAUNE (156.0) vs. Dirty Beers (111.7)

Result: Team Baune wins by 44.3
Mood: A full‑team eruption versus a roster that ran out of steam at the worst time.

Team Baune delivered their most complete performance of the season. Herbert (30.2) was electric, Cook III (26.4) dominated, and Sutton (20.6), Fannin Jr. (19.5), and McConkey (14.3) all hit with precision. Achane (18.0) added his usual burst, while Cam Little (11.0) provided stability. Even with a quiet Broncos D/ST (4.0), the lineup stacked points everywhere — a true semifinal statement.

Dirty Beers leaned on Warren (29.1) and Maye (23.7) to keep things competitive early, but the support never arrived. Amon‑Ra (9.4) and Judkins (10.1) underwhelmed, Brian Thomas Jr. (3.8) disappeared, and the Seahawks D/ST (-4.0) delivered a devastating negative. Jennings (18.1) and Henry (15.5) fought hard, but the ceiling simply wasn’t there to match Baune’s surge.

When your opponent hits everywhere, even your stars can’t save you.

Bench Regrets:
Team Baune: Doubs (19.4) and Shough (17.12) were strong options, but the starters were already perfect.
Dirty Beers: Perine (12.1) was the only meaningful upgrade — not nearly enough to close a 40‑point gap.

Team Baune storms into the Angry Bowl behind a complete, top‑to‑bottom explosion. Dirty Beers had bright spots, but the defensive collapse and FLEX struggles sealed their fate. In Round 16, you need stars and depth — Baune brought both.

🏅 The Battle for Third Place

Two teams fall just short of the Angry Bowl, but their journey isn’t over. The bracket narrows further as they collide in a chase for third place. Fueled by pride, frustration, and the need to finish the season on a high note. There’s no trophy here, but there’s plenty to prove.

One last chance to leave a mark.

💥 HOOLIGANS (128.08) vs. Team Hankson (101.82)

Result: Hooligans win by 26.26
Mood: A pride match where one team showed up swinging and the other never recovered from early collapse.

Hooligans came out firing behind massive performances from Stafford (30.88), McCaffrey (32.6), and Pickens (26.0). JSN (23.6) added a breakout of his own, while Hampton (16.5) and Kyren (11.5) kept the floor steady. The Steelers D/ST (4.0) and Loop (5.0) were quiet, and Ferguson (4.9) struggled, but the stars carried the load. When your QB, RB1, and WR2 all smash, the third‑place trophy starts leaning your way early.

Team Hankson never found their footing. Jaxson Dart’s shocking 0.02 cratered the lineup from the start, and Kelce (1.6) plus the Cowboys D/ST (-6.0) made the hole even deeper. Olave (36.8) was spectacular, Taylor (16.9) and Monangai (12.3) fought hard, and Higgins (14.3) chipped in — but the damage was already done. Fairbairn (16.0) did his part, yet the roster simply couldn’t overcome the early implosion.

When your opponent’s stars erupt and your QB gives you nothing, the script writes itself.

Bench Regrets:
Hooligans: Darnold (19.5) and Franklin (10.6) were nice-to-haves, but the starters were already rolling.
Team Hankson: Charbonnet (17.4), Wright (12.3), and Bo Nix (15.18) were meaningful upgrades — but none could erase the Dart disaster.

Hooligans close the season with a statement win, powered by elite performances from their core. Team Hankson had bright spots, but the QB collapse and defensive meltdown sealed their fate. Third place belongs to the Hooligans — a strong finish to a roller‑coaster season.

💥 TEAM GREENWELL (98.4) vs. Pray for Micah (90.8)

Result: Team Greenwell wins by 7.6
Mood: A meaningless matchup played with meaningful effort — because nobody wants to finish lower than their rival.

Team Greenwell rode a monster outing from Chase Brown (32.9) and a steady performance from Caleb Williams (21.0) to build a lead that held wire‑to‑wire. Jefferson (14.5) did his part, Mevis (13.0) chipped in, and Aaron Jones Sr. (11.3) added just enough. But it wasn’t all smooth — Marks (0.0), Rice (0.0), and Gadsden (1.7) tried their best to sabotage the effort. Thankfully, Brown did not allow nonsense today.

Pray for Micah had the talent to win but watched the engine sputter. Josh Allen (6.9) delivered a shocking dud, Javonte (6.3) never got going, and the Commanders D/ST (2.0) plus Myers (2.0) dragged the total down. Barkley (21.2), Etienne Jr. (16.6), Bowers (14.3), and Wilson (13.2) kept things respectable, but without QB production, the ceiling just wasn’t there.

When your opponent’s RB explodes and your QB faceplants, even meaningless games get decided quickly.

Bench Regrets:
Team Greenwell: Purdy (30.9) and McMillan (19.3) were massive upgrades — either one would’ve turned this into a comfortable blowout.
Pray for Micah: Prescott (19.16), Pitts Sr. (18.7), and Pierce (24.6) were huge missed swings that could’ve flipped the matchup entirely.

Team Greenwell walks away with the win and a slightly better final ranking — the true currency of the Consolation Ladder. Pray for Micah had the pieces, but the QB collapse sealed their fate. A meaningless game? Sure. But bragging rights last forever.
