🏈 Week 2 Fantasy Football Recap

🧰 HOOLIGANS (119.32) vs. Team Greenwell (89.58)

Result: Hooligans wins by 29.74
Mood: One team brought a Swiss army knife, the other brought a butter knife

Hooligans bounced back from Week 1 with a lineup that hit all the right notes — except at QB and D/ST. McCarthy posted a brutal 2.82, and the Colts defense barely showed up. But Kraft’s monster 24.4 at TE, Pickens’ 17.8, and McCaffrey doing McCaffrey things (22.7) kept the train rolling. Bucky Irving and Jacobs added solid RB depth, and Karty’s 9-point kicker line sealed the win.

Team Greenwell, meanwhile, had a full-on meltdown. Fields dropped a 3.98, Chase Brown underwhelmed, and Jefferson couldn’t carry the squad alone. Kamara and Prater were the only bright spots, but the rest of the lineup looked like it forgot to set an alarm.

One team overcame a QB disaster with depth and TE dominance. The other got caught in a fantasy fog and never found the exit.

Bench Regrets:
Hooligans: Stafford (17.32), Jennings (19.9), Ferguson (16.8) — 50+ points idle, but still a win.
Team Greenwell: Caleb Williams (16.98), McMillan (16.0) — could’ve made it competitive.

Hooligans didn’t just win — they won with a QB faceplant and a defense dud. That’s depth. Team Greenwell had the stars but forgot the spark. When your QB and WR1 combine for 15 and your bench is screaming for attention, it’s time to rethink the rotation. Next week: trust your bench, not your brand names.

GOAT (115.42) vs. 🔥 HUPHERS (122.9)

Result: HuPhers wins by 7.48
Mood: One team brought a flamethrower, the other brought a flashlight with dying batteries

HuPhers rode Lamar’s 26.3 and Chase’s nuclear 36.5 to a narrow win, despite a -5 from the Broncos D/ST that nearly sabotaged the whole operation. JSN popped off, Evans chipped in, and Bates booted a clean 9.0. It wasn’t flawless, but the stars showed up when it mattered.

GOAT had the depth — Javonte Williams exploded for 25.0, Jameson Williams dropped 18.8, and the Bills D/ST delivered a crisp 12. But Josh Allen fizzled with 11.82, Breece Hall vanished, and Bowers underwhelmed. The pieces were there, but the ceiling wasn’t.

One team rode a WR detonation and a QB heater. The other had depth but no ignition.

Bench Regrets:
GOAT: Dak (22.14), Etienne (16.9), Pitts (7.7) — 30+ point swing left idle.
HuPhers: Stevenson (21.2), Deebo (17.4), Allgeier (15.0), Johnson (15.9) — stacked bench.

HuPhers didn’t win clean — they won explosive. GOAT had the depth but missed the matchups. When your bench QB and RBs outscore your starters and you still lose by less than 8, it’s time to rethink the rotation. Next week: unleash the bench before it burns a hole in your win column.

One team rode kicker magic and WR consistency. The other got stuck in neutral with RB depth and WR disappointment.

Bench Regrets:
The Prison Wallets: Stroud (14.98), Packers D/ST (6.0) — no major regrets, just a flat lineup.

Team Parker didn’t win with stars — they won with strategy. The Prison Wallets had the QB and RB1, but the rest of the squad ghosted. When your kicker is your top scorer and you still lose, it’s time to check your WR depth and your FLEX instincts. Next week: start the legs that score, not the names that sell jerseys.
