🧱 HOOLIGANS (121.54) vs. Team Baune (85.32)

Result: Hooligans wins by 36.22
Mood: One team brought a toolbox and a blueprint, the other brought a pile of parts and no instructions

Hooligans delivered a balanced, high-floor performance across the board. McCaffrey did what McCaffrey does (24.0), Pickens popped for 17.8, and the Colts D/ST dropped a spicy 16. Karty booted a clean 15, and Stafford held steady with 13.84. Even with Kraft underwhelming and Jeudy disappearing, the lineup never cracked — it just kept scoring.

Team Baune, on the other hand, looked like a roster built for Week 1 that forgot Week 3 happened. Cook (20.8) and Achane (16.1) were solid, but Nabers vanished (3.3), Doubs flopped (4.5), and Boswell barely showed up. Mayfield did his job, but the supporting cast didn’t.

One team rode RB consistency and defensive punch. The other got lost in a WR wasteland.

Bench Regrets:
Hooligans: Ferguson (21.2) over Kraft; Calvin Austin (12.4) over Jeudy.
Team Baune: Sutton (23.8), Dobbins (15.3), Golden (10.1) — all outscored starters.

Hooligans didn’t just win — they built a foundation and let the points stack naturally.
