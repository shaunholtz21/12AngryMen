🏈 Week 17 — The Angry Bowl V
🏆 THE ANGRY BOWL V 🏆
Week 17  ·  Championship

It all comes down to Team Baune vs. Shoulda Played The Other Guy, proving once again that fantasy football is pure chaos.

The Road to the Angry Bowl is finally upon us.

Team Baune (91.82) vs. 💥 SHOULDA PLAYED THE OTHER GUY (152.18)

Result: Shoulda Played The Other Guy wins by 60.36
Mood: When one team brings a fire hose and the other brings a sparkler.

Team Baune came into the Angry Bowl looking for one more surge, but the lineup never found its footing. Shough (21.92) delivered a strong performance, and Harold Fannin Jr. (11.0) held up his end, but the supporting cast couldn't keep pace. Achane (14.2) and Cook (8.7) landed below expectations, the WR group combined for just 13.7 points, and the Broncos D/ST (7.0) couldn't swing momentum.

Shoulda Played The Other Guy showed up like it was personal. Derrick Henry detonated for 45.6 — the kind of championship swing that ends arguments — while McBride (23.6) and Wan'Dale Robinson (22.3) piled on. It was a complete, top-to-bottom title-winning performance.

Sometimes the Angry Bowl is a nail-biter. Sometimes it's a statement. This one was a statement.

Bench Regrets:
Team Baune: Cam Little (14.0) — wouldn't have changed the outcome.
Shoulda Played The Other Guy: Trevor Lawrence (23.12) and Quentin Johnston (14.8) — starters already sealed it.

Shoulda Played The Other Guy claims the Angry Bowl title with authority. Team Baune finishes as a worthy runner-up after a strong playoff run, but the trophy goes to Jeremy — and it wasn't close.

🎍 Trek to Irrelevance
Week 17  ·  Consolation

Games, mostly because the schedule says they have to.

The Trek to Irrelevance: one goal — don't sink any further.

Hooligans (106.66) vs. 💥 TEAM HANKSON (115.28)

Result: Team Hankson wins by 8.62
Mood: A classic "both teams played… but one played slightly more."

Hooligans had bright spots — CMC (28.1), Kyren Williams (16.0), and a solid 22.0 from Steelers D/ST — but Worthy's brutal 0.1 and Stafford (12.76) couldn't generate the ceiling needed.

Team Hankson leaned on Jaxson Dart (25.08), Chris Olave (25.9), and Jonathan Taylor (17.4). Even with quiet spots elsewhere the lineup held firm.

Not a shootout, not a meltdown — just a clean, narrow win for Team Hankson to close out the year.

Bench Regrets:
Team Hankson: Charbonnet (26.2) — starters handled business anyway.

💥 TEAM GREENWELL (109.1) vs. team spreitzer (92.98)

Result: Team Greenwell wins by 16.12
Mood: A "we're not done yet" performance vs. a "can this week end?" performance.

Team Greenwell closed with Caleb Williams (23.0) setting the tone and Chase Brown (29.1) absolutely erupting — the kind of late-season breakout that makes you wonder where this version was hiding all year.

team spreitzer had RJ Harvey (18.6), Zay Flowers (13.0), and Brandon Aubrey (16.0) keeping things respectable, but Jared Goff (2.08) never got off the ground.

Team Greenwell ends the year on a high note, while team spreitzer gets the fantasy equivalent of a polite shrug.

💥 THE MAX (110.1) vs. Pray for Michah (93.78)

Result: The Max wins by 16.32
Mood: When one team rides a superstar eruption and the other gets stuck in second gear.

The Max closed on a strong note — Bijan Robinson detonated for 39.9, Joe Burrow (20.4) kept things moving, and the Vikings D/ST (22.0) delivered a monster swing.

Pray for Michah had Josh Allen (23.18) doing heavy lifting and Michael Wilson (19.9) stepping up, but Barkley (6.8), the Commanders D/ST (0.0), and Brock Bowers (0.0) killed the ceiling.

The Max finishes the year with a statement win, while Pray for Michah gets a reminder that Week 17 can be cruel.

💥 TEAM CORBETT (87.1) vs. The Prison Wallets (49.9)

Result: Team Corbett wins by 37.2
Mood: One team played fantasy football; the other filed a missing persons report.

Team Corbett wrapped up with Devin Singletary (17.0) and Chris Godwin Jr. (23.8) carrying the offense. A much-needed exhale to close a tough season.

The Prison Wallets had a brutal finale — Jordan Love (0.0), DK Metcalf (0.0), and the Packers D/ST (-5.0). D'Andre Swift (21.9) was the lone bright spot. When your highest non-kicker scorer is your kicker, it's that kind of week.

Team Corbett ends the year with a rare victory lap, while The Prison Wallets get the fantasy equivalent of a "please try again later."
