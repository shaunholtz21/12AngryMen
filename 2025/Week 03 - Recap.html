🏈 Week 3 Fantasy Football Recap

🧱 HOOLIGANS (121.54) vs. Team Baune (85.32)

Result: Hooligans wins by 36.22
Mood: One team brought a toolbox and a blueprint, the other brought a pile of parts and no instructions

Hooligans delivered a balanced, high-floor performance across the board. McCaffrey did what McCaffrey does (24.0), Pickens popped for 17.8, and the Colts D/ST dropped a spicy 16. Karty booted a clean 15, and Stafford held steady with 13.84. Even with Kraft underwhelming and Jeudy disappearing, the lineup never cracked — it just kept scoring.

Team Baune, on the other hand, looked like a roster built for Week 1 that forgot Week 3 happened. Cook (20.8) and Achane (16.1) were solid, but Nabers vanished (3.3), Doubs flopped (4.5), and Boswell barely showed up. Mayfield did his job, but the supporting cast didn’t. When your WRs combine for 11.4 and your kicker posts 3, you’re not chasing wins — you’re chasing ghosts.

One team rode RB consistency and defensive punch. The other got lost in a WR wasteland.

Bench Regrets:
Hooligans: Ferguson (21.2) outscored Kraft by 15.3. Calvin Austin (12.4) would’ve doubled Jeudy’s output. Still a win.
Team Baune: Sutton (23.8), Dobbins (15.3), Golden (10.1) — all outscored starters. Sutton alone would’ve made it respectable.

Hooligans didn’t just win — they built a foundation and let the points stack naturally. Team Baune had the RBs and the potential, but the WRs ghosted and the bench screamed for attention. Next week: start Sutton, bench the ghosts, and maybe check the FLEX before kickoff.
