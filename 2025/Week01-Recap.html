🏈 Week 1 Fantasy Football Recap

Hooligans (111.42) vs. 🎆 SHOULDA PLAYED THE OTHER GUY (140.42)

Result: Shoulda Played The Other Guy wins by 29
Mood: One team brought fireworks, the other brought a sparkler

Shoulda Played The Other Guy drafted like they were trying to win Week 1 and Week 1 only — and it worked. Henry went full beast mode, Egbuka lit up the FLEX spot, and even the kicker dropped a 14-point flex bomb.

Hooligans had a solid foundation — McCaffrey and McCarthy did their jobs — but the rest of the lineup felt like it hit snooze. Worthy posted a zero, and the kicker might’ve been kicking Nerf balls.

One team beat projections by 20+. The other got ghosted by their WR2.

Bench Regrets:
Hooligans: No major regrets, but Stafford and Ferguson could’ve added a little polish.

Shoulda Played The Other Guy didn’t just win — they styled on ‘em. Starting Egbuka in Week 1 was either genius or madness, and it paid off like a lottery ticket. Hooligans? Solid bones, but they’ll need more than McCaffrey’s cape next week. Maybe check if Worthy’s cleats were even laced.

The Prison Wallets (104.42) vs. 🔥 HUPHERS (114.96)

Result: HuPhers wins by 10
Mood: One team brought a toolbox, the other brought a flamethrower

HuPhers didn’t dominate — they just hit the right buttons. Lamar went nuclear, Broncos D/ST dropped a surprise 16, and JSN showed up like he’d been waiting all offseason to prove a point. The rest of the lineup? A mix of “meh” and “maybe,” but it was enough.

The Prison Wallets had a solid floor — no disasters, no heroics. RBs were fine, WRs were fine, QB was fine. But when your defense scores 1 and your bench TE outscores your starter, “fine” doesn’t win matchups.

One team rode a QB heater and a surprise defense. The other played it safe and got burned by it.

Bench Regrets:
The Prison Wallets: Left 10 points on the table by starting Steelers D/ST over Packers. Freiermuth also outscored Kittle by 0.7.
HuPhers: Deebo Samuel exploded for 22.6 on the bench. Starting him over Chase or Evans would’ve made this a blowout.

HuPhers didn’t win with perfection — they won with timing. Lamar and the Broncos D/ST carried the day while The Prison Wallets quietly posted a respectable loss. But next week? Might be time to swap defenses and check if Kittle’s still the guy. And HuPhers? Maybe don’t leave Deebo in the garage again.

Dirty Beers (99.82) vs. 🌸 TEAM PARKER (106.72)

Result: Team Parker wins by 6.9
Mood: One team spilled their beer, the other sipped their way to victory

Team Parker didn’t blow the doors off — they just let Zay Flowers do the decorating. A monster 28.1 from the WR2 slot carried a lineup that otherwise looked like it was still stretching. Kincaid and Aubrey added just enough juice to push past the century mark.

Dirty Beers had Mahomes cooking, Pearsall popping, and then… a whole lot of undercooked sides. Hockenson flopped, Amon-Ra fizzled, and the RBs barely broke double digits.

One team rode a WR breakout and a rookie TE heater. The other left 40 points on the bench and paid the price.

Bench Regrets:
Dirty Beers: Tillman (16.2), Sampson (17.3), Odunze (15.7), and Henry all outscored starters. That’s a full buffet of regret.
Team Parker: No major misses. Bench was quiet.

Team Parker didn’t win with dominance — they won with Flowers. Dirty Beers had Mahomes and a bench full of fireworks, but forgot to light the fuse. When your top scorer is sitting in street clothes, it’s hard not to feel the sting. Next week: start your studs, not your brand names.

🐐 GOAT (129.86) vs. The Max (111.72)

Result: GOAT wins by 18.14
Mood: One team brought a stampede, the other brought a spreadsheet

GOAT didn’t need perfection — they had Josh Allen. Nearly 40 points from the QB slot set the tone, and Etienne, Breece Hall, and Butker followed suit. Even with a goose egg from the Giants D/ST, the rest of the lineup stacked points like clockwork.

The Max had flashes — Bijan balled out, Pittman Jr. delivered, and LaPorta looked like a TE1. But Burrow’s dud and a quiet day from Tyreek Hill kept the ceiling low.

One team rode a QB explosion and RB depth. The other got stuck in neutral with a star WR and a sleepy QB.

Bench Regrets:
GOAT: Javonte Williams (20.4) and Jayden Reed (13.5) both outscored starters.
The Max: Keenan Allen (19.8) and Palmer (11.1) could’ve made this a nail-biter.

GOAT didn’t just win — they let Josh Allen do the heavy lifting while the rest of the squad played support. The Max had the pieces, but left the heat on the bench. When your WR2 and FLEX combine for 38 and you still lose, it’s time to check your QB’s pulse and your lineup’s upside.

🎯 TEAM BAUNE (137.78) vs. Team Greenwell (126.72)

Result: Team Baune wins by 11.06
Mood: One team brought a balanced attack, the other brought a highlight reel and a headache

Team Baune didn’t need fireworks — they just hit every note. Baker Mayfield outperformed, James Cook and Achane ran wild, and Boswell booted a 15-point kicker line like it was 2013. Even with a quiet D/ST, the rest of the lineup stacked points like clockwork.

Team Greenwell had the flash — Fields dropped nearly 30, Jefferson and Conner held steady, and Goedert quietly did his job. But the supporting cast couldn’t keep pace, and the bench was screaming for attention.

One team rode steady starters and a kicker heater. The other left 60+ points on the bench and watched the win slip away.

Bench Regrets:
Team Baune: Herbert’s 27.92 would’ve outscored Mayfield, but not enough to change the outcome.
Team Greenwell: Garrett Wilson (22.5), Caleb Williams (24.2), and Hollywood Brown (19.9) all outscored starters. Swapping any two in would’ve flipped the result — this one hurts.

Team Baune didn’t win with flash — they won with fundamentals. Team Greenwell had the upside, but left it all on the bench. When your QB drops 29 and you still lose, it’s time to check your WR depth and your lineup decisions. Next week: trust your stars, not your projections.

Team Corbett (73.78) vs. 🦵 TEAM HANKSON (87.44)

Result: Team Hankson wins by 13.66
Mood: One team tripped over their own shoelaces, the other limped across the finish line

Team Hankson didn’t exactly light it up — Bo Nix face-planted, WRs fizzled, and the kicker barely showed up. But Kelce, Chuba, and Olave did just enough to keep the wheels on. It wasn’t pretty, but it was a win.

Team Corbett had Hurts doing his job, Lamb holding steady, and then… chaos. Pacheco vanished, Andrews ghosted, and the Ravens D/ST somehow posted a negative score. When your defense scores -7, you’re basically playing 10 vs. 11.

One team survived a QB flop and WR mediocrity. The other got sabotaged by their defense and a TE blackout.

Bench Regrets:
Team Corbett: Jakobi Meyers (17.7) and Geno Smith (17.48) both outscored starters. Swapping either in wouldn’t have saved the week, but it would’ve made the scoreboard less painful.
Team Hankson: Keon Coleman (25.2), Croskey-Merritt (14.2), and Charbonnet (10.7) all outscored starters. Leaving 50+ on the bench in a win? That’s a flex and a warning.

Team Hankson won ugly — but ugly still counts. Team Corbett had the QB, the WR1, and then a lineup that collapsed like a folding chair. When your defense scores negative and your TE posts 1.5, it’s hard to recover. Next week: bench the ghosts, start the grinders.
